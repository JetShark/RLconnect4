# Reinforcement Learning Assignment 2
Applied RL Tutorial Video
-  Due Date: March 30
-  Submission Format: Video link (YouTube or similar platform)
- Recommended Duration: 6–8 minutes

# Objective
Students will create a short tutorial video explaining an applied Reinforcement Learning (RL)
concept and demonstrating how it is used in a real-world problem.
The goal is for students to demonstrate that they can:
 - Explain a reinforcement learning concept clearly
 - Connect RL to a real application
 - Demonstrate how RL systems are implemented or simulated
 -Communicate technical ideas in a clear teaching format

Students may narrate using their own voice or a synthetic voice.

# Tutorial Video Structure
Your video should teach the concept clearly. It should include the following sections.
1. Problem Introduction (about 1 minute)

Explain the problem and why reinforcement learning is suitable for it.

Examples:
- Why RL is useful for autonomous driving
-S Why RL works well for trading agents or recommendation systems

2. RL Concepts (1–2 minutes)

Explain the basic RL formulation for your problem.

Include the following concepts where relevant:
 - Agent
 - Environment
 - State
 - Action
 - Reward
 - Policy

You may also briefly discuss exploration vs exploitation or value functions if relevant.

3. Method or Implementation (2–3 minutes)

Explain how RL is implemented for your problem.

You may show:
 - pseudocode or diagrams
 - an existing simulation environment
 - example training results
 - a code walkthrough

Optional:

Students may demonstrate their own implementation using Python, Gymnasium, Stable-Baselines,
PyTorch, or similar tools. A Jupyter notebook implementation is encouraged but not required.

4. Results or Example Behavior (about 1 minute)

Explain what the RL system learns.

Examples:
 - reward improvement over time
 - agent behavior in a simulation
 - how the policy changes during training
5. Challenges and Future Directions (30–60 seconds)

Briefly discuss limitations and current research challenges.

Examples:
- safety issues
- training instability
- reward design problems
- data efficiency

## Evaluation Rubric (100 Points)

- Concept Explanation – 25 points
- Clear explanation of the RL problem and its motivation. Correct use of RL terminology.
- Technical Understanding – 25 points
- Demonstrates understanding of how RL works for the selected problem. Accurately explains
methods or algorithms.
- Tutorial Clarity – 20 points
- Video clearly teaches the concept. Ideas are explained logically and visuals support understanding.
- Examples and Applications – 15 points
- Uses examples, simulations, case studies, or demonstrations to illustrate the RL system.
- Presentation Quality – 15 points
- Clear narration, readable visuals, and organized structure.

Bonus (Up to 10 Extra Points)
Students may receive bonus marks for deeper exploration such as:
• implementing their own RL experiment
• demonstrating training in a simulator
• comparing multiple RL algorithms
• exploring advanced RL approaches (e.g., Hierarchical RL or Meta-RL).

1. Reinforcement Learning for Playing Video Games
